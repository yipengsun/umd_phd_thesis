\section{Reconstructed samples}

In this sections inputs to reconstructed samples are described.
These reconstructed samples are grouped into two channels:
\Dstar channel, which contains \B candidates reconstructed in
$\Bzb \rightarrow \Dstarp (\rightarrow \Dz (\rightarrow \Km \pip) \pip) \mun \neumb$;
\Dz channel, which are reconstructed in
$\Bm \rightarrow \Dz (\rightarrow \Km \pip) \mun \neumb$.
The \Dz and \Dstar channels may have distinct requirements on their inputs
which are also briefly mentioned.

\subsection{Real data}

This analysis currently uses 1665~pb$^{-1}$ of proton-proton collision data
recorded by LHCb in 2016, at $\sqrt{s} = 13$~TeV.
It is planned to extend the use of data to 2016-2018 LHCb data.


\subsubsection{Nominal data sample}

For 2016, the nominal data are taken from Stripping 28r2.
Both the \Dstar and \Dz channels are reconstructed based on
\smalltt{Strippingb2D0MuXB2DMuForTauMuLine}.
The stripping line requires a \muon candidate forming a vertex with a high
$p_T$ $\Dz (\rightarrow \Km \pip)$ candidate, without any cut on the
invariant mass on the \Dz\mupm pair.
More detailed description on the stripping line can be found in
\cref{sec:selection:stripping}.

For \Dstar channel,
an additional slow \pip is required to form a vertex with \Dz and the invariant
mass of \Dz\pip pair is close to the measured (PDG) mass of \Dstarp.
These selections are applied offline.
The offline selections for both \Dstar and \Dz channel are detailed in
\cref{sec:selection:offline}.


\subsubsection{Wrong-sign samples}

The wrong-sign samples are used for studies of combinatorial backgrounds.
They are reconstructed from the same
\smalltt{Strippingb2D0MuXB2DMuForTauMuLine}
line, as both \Dz\mup and \Dz\mun are stripped.

More specifically, the wrong-sign \muon samples, referred as \B combinatorial
(\BComb), requires the wrong-sign \Dz\mup pairs;
the wrong-sign \pion samples, referred as \Dstar combinatorial (\DstComb),
exclusive to \Dstar channel,
requires a wrong-sign \Dz\pim pair,
with \Dz from the (right-sign) \Dz\mun candidates.
For more information about combinatorial backgrounds, refer to
\cref{sec:data-driven-templates:comb-bkg}.


\subsubsection{Fake muon sample}

The fake \muon samples are used for studying the misidentified (misID) \muon in
data.
They are constructed from
\smalltt{Strippingb2D0MuXFakeB2DMuForTauMuLine}\footnote{
    Note that this line uses a separate HLT2 trigger, which has a prescale of
    0.1. That is, only 10\% of events passing the HLT2 line is saved.

    The prescale factor can be found at
    \techurllink{https://gitlab.cern.ch/lhcb/Hlt/-/blob/hlt2016-patches/Hlt/Hlt2Lines/python/Hlt2Lines/XcMuXForTau/Lines.py}{cern-gitlab/Hlt}.
},
which has otherwise identical selections on \Dz
to the nominal stripping line
but explicitly requires the track $t$ that is combined with \Dz \emph{fails}
\muon identification requirement \isMuon.
That is,
the reconstructed pair in this line is $\Dz t^\pm$
where $t$ is not a \muon, instead of \Dz\mupm.
The misID studies are documented in
\cref{sec:data-driven-templates:mu-misid:unfold-fake-mu}.


\subsection{Simulation}

A large number of simulation (MC) samples is used to
model the signal, normalization, and partially reconstructed backgrounds in
data.
To save computation time, tracker-only (TO) MC, which has all but the tracking
system of the detector turned off (set to passive material), are used.
These MC samples are produced in Sim09k simulation condition, separate by years
(2015, 2016, 2017, 2018) and polarities (MagUp, MagDown), using
\pythia{8} generator.
Additional small full simulation (FullSim) samples are produced in Sim09j
condition for year 2016 to aid emulations of the missing detector responses
(mainly trigger).
The emulation procedures are discussed in \cref{sec:emulation-for-to-mc}.

Both TO MC and FullSim samples are loosely filtered to keep events in LHCb
acceptance, but without filtering on stripping line selections.
The filtering selections are listed in \cref{tab:gen-cut}, and are referred as
generator level cuts.

MC samples used for both \Dz and \Dstar channels are listed in
\cref{tab:mc-d0-dst}, with TO and FullSim numbers listed separately.
Some samples contribute to both channels due to feed down\footnote{
    This means that the reconstruction algorithm was unable to find the
    additional slow \pion required to reconstruct a \Dstar, thus only a
    \Dz was constructed and the candidate is \emph{feed down} from \Dstar
    channel to \Dz channel.
}, and are indicated in the same table.

The heavy $D^{**}$ and $DDX$ MC are cocktails, with the same compositions as
listed in \cite{LHCb-ANA-2020-056}.
The heavy $D^{**}$ MC suffer from the problem that the $\piz \piz$ decays
are not simulated, inconsistent of the isospin limit.
The $\piz \piz$ contribution is modelled by randomly selecting $\frac{1}{3}$ of
$\pip \pim$ candidates and treating them as \emph{isolated} regardless of their
isolation BDT scores.
The isolation BDT is briefly discussed in \cref{sec:selection:skims}.

The reconstructed particles are truth-matched to ensure they have the correct
true particle IDs per MC mode.
This effectively removes ghost and misidentified tracks, providing a consistent
modelling and ensuring orthogonality between MC samples.


\subsection{Additional control samples}
\label{sec:reconstructed-samples:add-ctrl}

A $\Bp \rightarrow \jpsi \Kp$ sample is used to derive $B$ kinematic and
multiplicity corrections to MC.
The data sample is reconstructed from the
\smalltt{StrippingBetaSBu2JpsiKDetachedLine}, and fitted with
a \sPlot procedure.
The MC sample used to compare with $\jpsi \Kp$ data is listed in
\cref{tab:add-mc-samples}.
The procedure is described in \cref{sec:data-mc:init-rwt:jpsi-k-correction}.

A simulated $\Dz \rightarrow \Km \pip$ MC sample,
also listed in \cref{tab:add-mc-samples},
is requested to model the momentum smearing in the misID sample due to
decay-in-flight effect, which is described in
\cref{sec:data-driven-templates:mu-misid:dif}.
